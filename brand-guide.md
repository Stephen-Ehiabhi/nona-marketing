# Brand Guide: Nona
> Last updated: 2026-03-11

## Brand Identity
- **Name:** Nona
- **Tagline:** Scan. Plan. Cook.
- **Mission:** Eliminate the daily "what should I cook?" stress by turning your actual kitchen into smart, personalized meals.
- **Vision:** A world where no food goes to waste and every home cook feels confident.
- **Core Values:** Simplicity, Intelligence, Intentionality, Warmth, Inclusivity
- **Brand Archetype:** The Sage meets The Caregiver — calm authority that genuinely helps.

## Target Audience

### Primary Persona: "The Overwhelmed Weeknight Parent"
- **Demographics:** Age 30-42, families with kids, $50K-$100K household income, urban/suburban
- **Psychographics:** Values feeding kids well, hates food waste, feels guilty about delivery, defaults to same 7 meals
- **Pain Points:** Standing at fridge at 5:30pm with no plan, throwing away spoiled produce, Uber Eats bills
- **Goals:** Cook more at home, feed family well without the mental load, reduce waste
- **Platform Behavior:** Instagram food accounts, TikTok #WhatICookForDinner, Reddit r/MealPrepSunday, Facebook dinner groups
- **Purchase Triggers:** Throwing away food, seeing delivery spend, Yummly shutting down, back-to-school "cook more" resolution
- **Purchase Barriers:** "Does it actually work on my messy fridge?", another subscription, privacy concerns

### Secondary Persona: "The Adventurous Solo Cook"
- **Demographics:** Age 24-35, solo or couple, cities, aesthetically driven early adopter
- **Psychographics:** Cooking as creative expression, interested in global cuisines beyond Western default, design-conscious
- **Pain Points:** Bored cooking rotation, buys interesting ingredients with no plan, wants Nigerian jollof not just pasta
- **Goals:** Culinary discovery, expand repertoire, cook confidently with unfamiliar cuisines

### Tertiary Persona: "The Health-Conscious Optimizer"
- **Demographics:** Age 26-38, tracks macros, quantified self mindset
- **Pain Points:** Meal prep tedium, canceling HelloFresh, needing nutrition to match fitness goals
- **Goals:** Hit protein targets, reduce processed food, maximize nutrition with minimal effort

## Brand Voice

### Voice Attributes
| Attribute | What It Means | Example |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| Conversational | Like a smart friend, not a corporation. Short sentences. Real talk. | "You have food. You just don't know what to make with it." |
| Confident | Makes bold claims, backs them up. Doesn't hedge. | "92% pantry match on average. Zero grocery runs." |
| Warm | Empathetic to the daily struggle. Never condescending. | "That sad bag of spinach? Gone era." |

### Tone Spectrum
- **Educational mode:** Patient, clear, zero jargon. "Think of it as a chef standing in your kitchen."
- **Entertainment mode:** Witty, relatable, self-aware. "Works on messy fridges too. We checked."
- **Promotional mode:** Benefit-first, urgency without sleaze. "Try free for 7 days. No credit card."
- **Crisis mode:** Transparent, brief, solution-oriented.

### Vocabulary
- **Words/phrases TO USE:** "tonight's dinner", "right now", "actually", "your kitchen", "real", "every night", "zero guesswork"
- **Words/phrases to AVOID:** "revolutionary", "disruptive", "leverage", "synergy", "unlock your potential", "game-changer", corporate jargon
- **Jargon policy:** Never. If AI is mentioned, it's what it does, not what it is.
- **Emoji policy:** Minimal. None in headlines. Sparingly in social.
- **Punctuation style:** Casual. Fragments OK. Dashes and periods over exclamation marks.

### Voice Examples
**On-brand:** "You bought groceries three days ago and already forgot what's in there. Something's expiring. Probably."
**Off-brand:** "Our revolutionary AI-powered solution leverages computer vision to optimize your meal planning experience!"

## Visual Identity

- **Primary Color:** Black #000000
- **Secondary Color:** White #FFFFFF
- **Neutral Scale:** Gray 50-900 (#FAFAFA to #171717)
- **Accent Color:** None. Monochrome only. Food photos provide the only color.
- **Typography — Heading:** Fraunces (Bold 800) — warm, rounded serif. Editorial, magazine-feel.
- **Typography — Body:** Plus Jakarta Sans (Regular 400, SemiBold 600) — modern, approachable.
- **Photography Style:** Minimal, clean. The app UI and real food are the hero, not stock photos.
- **Design Philosophy:** Mibu aesthetic — extremely minimal, generous whitespace, intentional simplicity. When in doubt, remove.
- **Visual DON'Ts:** No color accents in UI chrome. No filled icons. No gradients. No drop shadows on cards. No decorative borders. Never smaller than 11px text.

## Key Messaging

- **Primary Value Proposition:** Point your camera at your kitchen. Nona plans your meals with AI.
- **Elevator Pitch:** You know that moment at 5:30pm when you're staring into a full fridge with no idea what to cook? Nona fixes that. Scan your fridge, get three meals you can cook right now — personalized to your taste, your diet, and 35+ cuisines. Then it walks you through cooking step by step.
- **Unique Differentiators:**
  1. Full-stack: scan → inventory → recommendation → guided cooking → shopping list (no other app does the whole pipeline)
  2. 35+ cuisines including underrepresented African, Southeast Asian, Latin cuisines
  3. Mibu minimal design — stands out in a sea of cluttered food apps
- **Proof Points:** 500+ beta testers, 40% less food waste reported, 92% pantry match rate, Yummly gap (20M+ displaced users)
- **Positioning Statement:** "For busy families who are tired of the nightly 'what should I cook?' stress, Nona is the AI kitchen assistant that sees what's in your fridge and instantly recommends personalized meals you can actually cook tonight — unlike Samsung Food or SuperCook that just give you a recipe database to search through."
